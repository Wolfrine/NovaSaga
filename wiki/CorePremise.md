# The Central Record

## The World After the Event

The records describe a world in which a rare cosmic or planetary disturbance disrupts electricity and weakens the barriers around hidden Luminary places. After the [[Events|Event]], ordinary communities face scarcity and disorder while older powers become harder to deny.

The first movement of the account remains close to ordinary life: roads, homes, families, local fear, public disorder, and the strain of service when familiar systems fail.

## The Hidden Tradition

The [[Factions|Luminaries]] are hidden, restrained, and concerned with balance. They do not appear in the records as conquerors or public miracle-workers. They are associated instead with perception, discipline, aura, resonance, and careful intervention.

Luminary power depends on restraint and inner alignment. The stronger accounts describe subtle influence over emotion, environment, perception, and charged matter rather than spectacle.

## Concealment as Containment

Luminary concealment is not only a response to fear or disbelief. The religion-context record preserves a stricter reason: direct truth can change the mind receiving it. Before humanity is ready, open knowledge that consciousness, intent, emotion, belief, and inner alignment affect the world can turn consciousness into a weapon.

The [[Factions|Luminary]] method is therefore indirect. Symbol, story, ritual, vow, taboo, festival, relic, place, and moral teaching shape responsibility without exposing the full machinery.

## The Conflict Beneath Collapse

Dark forces are linked with fear, anger, decay, corruption, and accumulated negative energy. They threaten both the visible world and the hidden sanctuaries.

Several accounts begin with ordinary companions drawn into a weakened hidden realm through storm, river, and accident. Their value is not first described as power, but as conduct under collapse: service, loyalty, courage, and the willingness to protect others.

## Unsettled Edges

The number and composition of the first companion group remain unsettled. Older notes often mention four or five boys, but no stable names or lineages have been recorded.

The role of portals, extraterrestrial radiants, and interplanetary contact remains outside the central record. These may belong to later accounts, symbolic traditions, or deprecated expansions.

The scope of the religious dimension also remains open. The core record holds that Luminaries guide through indirect context-setting; what remains unclear is whether this touched all religions, selected traditions, symbolic practices only, higher entities, human inventions, or mixed histories.
